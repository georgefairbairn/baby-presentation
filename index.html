🤰 Things That Can Go Wrong

💔 Miscarriage

1 in 8 known pregnancies, 80% in first 12 weeks. mostly chromosomal - not anything you did.

⚠️ Ectopic

1 in 90 pregnancies. signs: sharp one-sided pain, shoulder tip pain, dizziness. needs immediate attention.

🤕 Preeclampsia

after week 20. sudden swelling, severe headaches, vision changes. more common in first pregnancies.

🍭 Gestational Diabetes

mid-second trimester, often no symptoms. picked up by routine screening. manageable.

🤰 Things Outside Our Control

🧬 Chromosomal Abnormalities

cause ~50% of miscarriages - happen by chance, not your fault

📈 Risk Drops Over Time

80% occur in first 12 weeks - risk at 12 wks is 1.7%, by 16 wks just 0.5%

💓 Heartbeat = Good Sign

risk drops significantly once heartbeat is detected

🌟 85% Have a Healthy Pregnancy After

even after two or three, 75% still succeed

💛 Bottom Line

focus on what's in our control. be kind to ourselves about what isn't.

💰 Maternity Leave & EI

🇨🇦 How EI Works

✅ We Both Qualify

600+ insurable hours each - EI replaces 55% of earnings

📈 $729/week Cap (2026)

we'll both hit it - it's taxable, so plan ahead

💕 Maternity Leave

💕 15 Weeks - Just for You

birth parent only, cannot be shared

📅 When Can It Start?

up to 12 weeks before due date, must end 17 weeks after birth

Apply Early: Apply as soon as you stop working - waiting 4+ weeks may lose benefits. Apply for maternity and parental at the same time.

💰 Parental Leave Options

Can be shared between both parents. Both must choose the same option - no switching once payments start.

✅ Standard Option

📅 Up to 35 Weeks Each

max 40 weeks shared, 55% income ($729/week cap)

👨 5 Weeks Use-It-or-Lose-It

reserved exclusively for me - free money if I take it

🕐 Extended Option

📅 Up to 61 Weeks Each

max 69 weeks shared, 33% income ($437/week cap)

👨 8 Weeks Use-It-or-Lose-It

reserved exclusively for me - free money if I take it

Tips: Check with HR about EI top-ups. Apply for the Canada Child Benefit at birth for tax-free monthly income.
